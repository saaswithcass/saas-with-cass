## The Honest Downsides

I'd be doing you a disservice if I didn't mention the challenges:

**1. It requires discipline.** A CRM is only as good as the data you put in. If your team doesn't consistently log calls, add notes, and update deal stages, you won't get value.

**2. You have to design your own process.** HighLevel doesn't force you into a specific workflow. That's powerful but means you need to think through your own process first.

**3. Advanced customization takes work.** If you want deeply custom automations, you might need to understand webhooks and workflow logic.

These aren't flaws with HighLevel—they're inherent to any powerful CRM. But it's worth knowing upfront.
